<p><p>The solid-state overload relays market is primarily divided into thermal and magnetic overload relays. Thermal overload relays use heat-sensitive materials to detect current overloads, providing protection by opening the circuit when excess heat is generated. In contrast, magnetic overload relays rely on magnetic fields to sense overload conditions, offering rapid response and better performance under dynamic loads. Both types are essential for safeguarding electrical systems, but they serve different applications based on response time, sensitivity, and installation needs.</p></p>
