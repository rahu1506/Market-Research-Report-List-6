<p><p>Weakly Basic Anion Exchange Resins are categorized into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I resins are primarily based on a tertiary amine functional group, which allows them to effectively remove weak acids and anions from solutions. Type II resins, on the other hand, utilize a primary or secondary amine, offering higher efficiency for stronger acids and a broader pH operating range. The choice between these types depends on specific application requirements in water treatment and chemical processing.</p></p>
