<p><p>The engineered wood siding market encompasses various types, including Oriented Strand Board (OSB), High-Density Fiberboard (HDF), and Veneered Plywood. OSB consists of strands of wood compressed and bonded with adhesive, offering durability and cost-effectiveness. HDF, created from wood fibers combined under heat and pressure, is known for its smooth surface and versatility. Veneered Plywood features a core layer of plywood topped with a veneer, providing an attractive finish while maintaining structural integrity. Each type caters to different aesthetic and performance needs.</p></p>
