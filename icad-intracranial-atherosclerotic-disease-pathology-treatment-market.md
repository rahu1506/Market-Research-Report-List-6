<p><p>Intracranial Atherosclerotic Disease (ICAD) involves the narrowing of blood vessels in the brain due to plaque buildup, leading to an increased risk of stroke. Treatment options include stenting and percutaneous transluminal angioplasty (PTA) balloon procedures. Stenting involves placing a mesh tube to keep arteries open, while PTA balloon therapy uses a balloon catheter to dilate narrowed vessels. Both methods aim to restore blood flow and reduce neurological complications, contributing to the growing market driven by advancements in neurovascular interventions.</p></p>
