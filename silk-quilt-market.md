<p><p>The Silk Quilt market primarily features two types of silk: Mulberry silk and Tussah silk. Mulberry silk, produced by silkworms that primarily feed on mulberry leaves, is known for its smooth texture, luster, and durability, making it a premium choice for luxury quilts. Tussah silk, derived from wild silkworms, offers a coarser texture and a more natural, earthy appearance, appealing to eco-conscious consumers. Both types cater to diverse preferences, influencing market dynamics and pricing in the silk quilt industry.</p></p>
