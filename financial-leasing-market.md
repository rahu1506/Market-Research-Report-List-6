<p><p>Financial leasing is a contractual arrangement where a lessee obtains the right to use an asset, such as equipment or property, without owning it, while the lessor retains ownership. This arrangement offers businesses flexibility in managing cash flow and accessing advanced equipment.</p><p>The Financial Leasing Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for asset financing, particularly among small and medium-sized enterprises. <p><p>Financial leasing involves various market types catering to different needs. Sale and leaseback allows companies to sell assets and lease them back for liquidity. Direct leasing involves lessors purchasing assets and leasing them directly to lessees. Leveraged leases use debt to finance an asset, sharing risks and benefits. Straight leases offer fixed payments over the lease term, while modified leases allow for flexibility in terms. The primary lease market involves new leases, while the secondary market deals with existing lease agreements.</p></p>
