<p><strong>The Carbon Fiber For Civil Aviation Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PAN-Based Carbon Fiber</li><li>Viscose-Based Carbon Fiber</li><li>Pitch-Based Carbon Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Carbon Fiber for Civil Aviation market primarily includes three types: PAN-based, Viscose-based, and Pitch-based carbon fibers. PAN-based carbon fibers, derived from polyacrylonitrile, offer high tensile strength and thermal stability, making them ideal for structural components. Viscose-based carbon fibers, produced from regenerated cellulose, are more cost-effective and suitable for non-structural applications. Pitch-based carbon fibers, obtained from petroleum or coal tar pitches, provide excellent thermal and chemical resistance, fitting niche applications in specialized aviation components. Each type contributes uniquely to enhancing aircraft performance and efficiency.</p></p>
