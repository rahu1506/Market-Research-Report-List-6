<p><p>The Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis (IPF) drug market is categorized into three main types: glucocorticoids, immunosuppressive agents, and others. Glucocorticoids are anti-inflammatory medications used to reduce lung inflammation, while immunosuppressive agents work by dampening the immune response to prevent further lung damage. The "others" category encompasses a variety of treatments, such as antifibrotic drugs, which specifically target the fibrosis process. Together, these options aim to manage symptoms, slow disease progression, and improve quality of life for IPF patients.</p></p>
