The shift toward electric vehicles (EVs) poses a challenge to the market, but the demand for crankshafts in hybrid vehicles and heavy-duty applications continues to support growth. <p><p>The crankshaft market includes various types based on material and manufacturing processes. **Cast Iron Crankshafts** are cost-effective and widely used in low-to-moderate performance engines due to their durability and ease of production. **Nodular Iron Crankshafts** offer enhanced strength and flexibility, making them suitable for higher-performance applications. **Forged Steel Crankshafts** are robust, durable, and ideal for high-stress environments like racing or heavy-duty engines. **Billet Steel Crankshafts**, machined from solid steel, provide superior precision and strength, catering to extreme performance and custom engine builds. Each type serves specific performance and application needs.</p></p>
