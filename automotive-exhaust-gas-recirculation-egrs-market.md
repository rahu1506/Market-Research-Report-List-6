<p><p>Automotive Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) is an emission control technology used in internal combustion engines to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. It works by recirculating a portion of the engine's exhaust gas back into the combustion chamber, lowering the combustion temperature and reducing the formation of harmful pollutants. EGR systems are widely adopted in both gasoline and diesel engines to meet stringent environmental regulations.</p><p>The Automotive Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing global emission standards and the rising demand for fuel-efficient vehicles. Additionally, the shift toward electric vehicles (EVs) is influencing the market, as manufacturers explore hybrid solutions to bridge the gap between internal combustion engines and fully electric powertrains. Overall, the EGR market is poised for robust growth, supported by regulatory pressures and technological innovations.</p></p>
