<p><ul><li>Analog Type</li><li>Digital Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive height sensor market is primarily divided into two types: analog and digital. Analog height sensors use variable resistive technology to provide continuous signals based on vehicle height changes, offering a simpler design and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, digital height sensors utilize advanced microcontroller technology to deliver precise measurements and enhanced data processing, allowing for better vehicle performance and safety features. Both types address critical functions in automotive applications, including ride height adjustments and stability control.</p></p>
