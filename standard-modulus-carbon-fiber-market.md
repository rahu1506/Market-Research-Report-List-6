<p><p>Standard modulus carbon fiber is categorized based on the number of filaments in the yarn, which directly influences strength and application. The 1K type consists of 1,000 filaments, ideal for lightweight applications, while 3K (3,000 filaments) is suitable for automotive and sporting goods. The 6K (6,000 filaments) variant offers a balance of strength and weight for varied uses. The 12K (12,000 filaments) is used in larger structures, while above 12K fibers are utilized in industrial applications requiring high strength and durability.</p></p>
