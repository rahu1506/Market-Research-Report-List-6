<p><p>The aluminum alloys market is primarily divided into two types: wrought alloys and cast alloys. Wrought alloys are processed by shaping and deforming the metal, resulting in products like sheets, plates, and extrusions, which are known for their strength and lightweight properties. In contrast, cast alloys are produced by pouring molten aluminum into molds, allowing for complex shapes and designs, often used in automotive and aerospace industries. Both types cater to diverse applications, driven by their unique mechanical characteristics.</p></p>
