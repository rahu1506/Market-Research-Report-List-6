<p><p>The bio-surfactants market encompasses three primary types: anionic, cationic, and amphoteric surfactants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, making them effective for detergents and cleaning products. Cationic surfactants, with a positive charge, are commonly used in fabric softeners and antimicrobial agents. Amphoteric surfactants possess both positive and negative charges, providing versatility across various applications, including personal care and pharmaceuticals. The growing demand for eco-friendly alternatives drives innovation and expansion in these bio-surfactant categories.</p></p>
